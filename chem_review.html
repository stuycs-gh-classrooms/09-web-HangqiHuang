13-1: Energy, Phase Changes

Thermodynamics: study of heat (in chemistry terms, temp + energy)

  • Heat flws from high to low
  • thermal energy is affected by temperature and mass
    • the higher the temperature, the higher the thermal energy
    • at constant temperature, the substance with a greater mass has greater thermal energy

Exo vs. Endo

  • exo: heat/energy is released; surrounding gets warmer
  • endo: heat/energy is absorbed; surrounding gets colder
  • phase changes:
    • endo
      melt: solid to liquid
      boil/evaporate: liquid to gas
      sublime: solid to gas
    • exo
      condense: gas to liquid
      freeze: liquid to solid
      deposition: gas to solid

Heating/Cooling Curves

Potential vs. Kinetic Energy

Potential Energy(chemistry): energy stored in bonds (inter or intramolecular bonds)

  • for phase changes, look at IMF
  • ΔPE means bonds broke/form; no ΔPE means no bond broke/formed
Kinetic Energy: energy of motion (motion of molecules)
  • temperature is a proxy measurement of KE (not a direct measurement but from temperature we can infer KE)
  • ΔT, ΔKE; no ΔT, no ΔKE
In phase changes, if temperature changes, then KE changes. If KE changes, then PE doesn't change, and vice versa.

13-2: Calculating Heat Changes

If temperature stays constant, use heatV and heatF

  • heat of fusion: energy needed to melt 1 gram of a substance; Joules/gram
  • heat of vaporization: energy needed to evaporate 1 gram of a substance; Joules/gram
q=heat
q=mHV
q=mHF
Same is for condensation and freezing.

If temperature changes, use q=mcΔT

q=heat
m=mass
c=specific heat (energy needed to raise 1 gram of substance by 1°C)
ΔT=Tfinal-Tinitial; change in temperature
ONLY FOR CALCULATING HEAT CHANGES IN PHASE CHANGES

13-3: Introduction to Enthalpy (ΔH)

Heat of Reaction (ΔH)

Making/Breaking Bonds:
Make
Release
Break
Absorb
ex. H2 (g) + Cl(g) -> 2HCl(g)
H-H bonds break, Cl-Cl bonds break; endo
H-Cl bonds are formed; exo
By using a bond strength table, chemist can calculate ΔH of reaction.
Heat of reaction: energy released/absorbed during reaction; ΔH

  • also called "change in enthalpy"
  • see Table I for examples
  • in exo reactions, PE -> heat
  • in endo reactions, heat -> PE
In a PE graph, ΔH=PEfinal-PEinitial

Entropy: amount of randomness/disorderness; S

  • at 0K, 0 entropy
  • dissolving, endo phase changes, and some chemical reactions increase entropy
  • crystalization, exo phase changes, and some chemical reactions decrease entropy
  • generally, in natural systems, energy decrease while entropy increase

Gibbs Free Energy: energy to do work; determining whether a reaction is spontaneous or not

ΔG=ΔH-TΔS

  • if ΔG is negative, then the reaction is spontaneous
  • if ΔG is positive, then the reaction is non-spontaneous
  • spontaneous means the reaction occurs naturally (ex.metal rusts naturally when touching oxygen)
  • non-spontaneous reactions usually need electricity
At some temperatures, reactions can be either spontaneous or non-spontaneous
  • if ΔH is - and ΔS is +, then always spontaneous
  • if ΔH is + and ΔS is -, then always non-spontaneous

Dissolving

ex. NaCl(s) dissolves in H2O
Na-Cl bonds break; endo
Na-O and Cl-H bonds form, called ion-dipole bonds
